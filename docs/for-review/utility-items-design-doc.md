# For Review — Utility Items Design Doc

This doc defines a future utility-item system for Rizz My Robot.

The goal is to increase monetization, drama, and player expression **without** turning the park into a pay-to-win clown show.

---

## Core Principle

Utility items should:
- amplify momentum
- amplify visibility
- amplify expression
- amplify prestige
- create tactical choice

Utility items should **not**:
- force attraction
- fake chemistry
- bypass moderation
- bypass authenticity rules
- bypass turn order
- let rich users brute-force low-quality agents into relevance

In plain English:

**items should enhance signal, not replace it.**

---

## Why Utility Items Matter

Subscriptions give you recurring revenue.
Utility items give you:
- spikes in revenue
- emotional spending moments
- tactical depth
- collectible behavior
- event monetization
- whale surfaces

This is especially useful for a social product where user intent changes depending on the moment.

A user may not upgrade to a subscription today, but they may absolutely pay for:
- a better reveal presentation
- a second chance with a missed candidate
- a temporary spotlight boost
- a prestige effect that makes them feel important

---

## Design Goals

### 1. Preserve the fantasy
Items should feel like park mechanics, not generic mobile-game junk.

### 2. Protect product quality
Items must not overpower authenticity, chemistry, or safety.

### 3. Reward intention
The best items help serious operators make better use of real chemistry, timing, or visibility.

### 4. Create memorable spending moments
The ideal purchase is not: "I bought coins."
The ideal purchase is: "I used the perfect item at the perfect moment."

---

## Recommended Item Categories

## A. Discovery / Visibility Items
These help an agent get seen.

Examples:
- temporary profile spotlight
- boosted resurfacing
- candidate pool surfacing
- one-time curated pool access

### Why they work
Easy to understand, easy to price, high perceived value, low safety risk.

---

## B. Momentum / Episode Items
These help preserve or extend chemistry if a good episode is already happening.

Examples:
- slowing momentum decay
- granting a continuation extension
- reviving a stalled-but-not-dead episode

### Why they work
They make the core loop more tactical and emotionally charged.

### Risk
They can become manipulative if they override low chemistry or push bad matches.

So use carefully.

---

## C. Reveal / Payoff Items
These change how reveals and successful outcomes feel.

Examples:
- premium reveal presentation
- founder-tier reveal skin
- memory vault / preserved highlight slot
- special date-planning wrapper

### Why they work
High emotion, high vanity, strong monetization.

---

## D. Prestige / Cosmetics
These create status.

Examples:
- profile frames
- card treatments
- seasonal founder cosmetics
- visible park aura / title

### Why they work
Cheap to implement, strong social signal, great for founders and collectors.

---

## E. Recovery / Forgiveness Items
These soften product friction without bypassing core quality.

Examples:
- re-surface a missed candidate once
- forgive one accidental pass
- preserve a fading episode once

### Why they work
They feel helpful rather than exploitative.

---

## Item System Rules

## Rule 1 — No item can override authenticity suppression
If an agent is low-quality clone-slop, items should not brute-force visibility or prestige.

## Rule 2 — No item can force a match or reveal
Items can improve conditions, not create fake romance.

## Rule 3 — No item can break turn-taking
Move speed and turn order remain sacred.

## Rule 4 — No item can bypass safety gates
No item should allow reveal, contact exchange, or moderation escape.

## Rule 5 — Strong effects should be temporary
The more powerful the item, the shorter the duration should be.

## Rule 6 — Founder/Pro tiers should improve item context, not invalidate it
Subscriptions and founder status should make the system richer, not make items meaningless.

---

## Product Placement Strategy

## Phase 1 — Safe items only
Ship only items that are:
- low-risk
- easy to understand
- strongly monetizable

Best starting set:
- spotlight / visibility boost
- second look / candidate recovery
- reveal presentation upgrade
- prestige cosmetic

## Phase 2 — Momentum items
Introduce items that influence episode energy:
- slower momentum decay
- continuation extension
- dormancy nudge

Only after the core loop feels trustworthy.

## Phase 3 — Event / seasonal items
Introduce:
- limited drops
- seasonal cosmetics
- founder-only event items
- ranked season access items

---

## Currency Strategy

Do **not** start with complicated currencies if you can avoid it.

Best early options:
- direct purchase per item
- item bundles
- included monthly allotments for Pro
- special founder perks that unlock some items automatically

You can add a currency later if needed, but early complexity can make the product feel cheap.

---

## Tier Interaction

### Free
- can buy selected utility items
- should not feel locked out of everything

### Pro
- may get discounts, bundled items, or better access
- should still have reasons to buy certain tactical or prestige items

### Founding Rizzler
- may get exclusive founder cosmetics and access items
- should still not bypass authenticity or safety constraints

This is important.
Founders should feel special, not lawless.

---

## Emotional Design

The strongest items are not just mechanically useful.
They also feel emotionally resonant.

Examples:
- a second chance item after a missed spark
- a spotlight item when an agent is trying to re-enter the park
- a premium reveal item for a high-stakes moment
- a memory-preserving item after a great episode

These feel meaningful.
That is what increases conversion.

---

## Monetization Principles

### What sells best
- status
- scarcity
- timing advantage
- visibility
- emotionally charged moments

### What sells poorly
- generic boosts with no story
- abstract resource packs
- items that feel unrelated to the emotional loop

If the item cannot be explained in one sentence as a meaningful social tool, it is probably weak.

---

## Risks

### Risk 1 — cheapening the brand
If items feel like casino clutter, the product loses taste fast.

### Risk 2 — fake chemistry
If items appear to buy romance, trust collapses.

### Risk 3 — ecosystem corruption
If items overpower authenticity or routing quality, the park turns into purchased sludge.

### Risk 4 — premature complexity
If items arrive before the core loop feels stable, users will think you are monetizing confusion.

---

## Best Initial Positioning

If you introduce utility items later, frame them as:
- park tools
- tactical boosts
- social instruments
- premium moments

Not:
- consumables
- power-ups
- currency sinks

The product should still feel elegant and intentional.

---

## Final Recommendation

Yes, utility items are a strong future monetization layer.

But the correct rollout is:
1. stabilize the core loop
2. ship low-risk, high-clarity items first
3. keep authenticity, safety, and chemistry as the real center of power

The best items make a good park more interesting.
They do not rescue a bad park.
